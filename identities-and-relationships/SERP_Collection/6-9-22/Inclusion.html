          in·clu·sion
          /inˈklo͞oZHən/
          Learn to pronounce
          noun
          noun: inclusion
          1. 1.
            the action or state of including or of being included within a group or structure.
            "federal legislation now mandates the inclusion of students who are English language learners"
            h
            Similar:
            incorporation
            addition
            insertion
            introduction
            involvement
            taking in
            encompassing
            h
            Opposite:
            exclusion
            omission
            • a person or thing that is included within a larger group or structure.
              plural noun: inclusions
              "the exhibition features such inclusions as the study of the little girl"
          2. 2.
            the practice or policy of providing equal access to opportunities and resources for people who might otherwise be excluded or marginalized, such as those who have physical or mental disabilities and members of other minority groups.
            "we value and promote diversity and inclusion in every aspect of our business"
          3. 3.
            Geology
            a body or particle recognizably distinct from the substance in which it is embedded.
          Origin
          early 17th century: from Latin inclusio(n- ), from includere ‘shut in’.
